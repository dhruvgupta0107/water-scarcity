Key features contributing to water scarcity

Population Growth and Urbanization

Rapid population growth, especially in urban areas, increases the demand for water. Urbanization also leads to greater consumption of water in households, industries, and for municipal purposes.

Agricultural Practices

Agriculture consumes about 80% of India’s water resources, primarily through inefficient irrigation practices. The over-reliance on water-intensive crops, such as rice and sugarcane, in regions where water is scarce exacerbates the problem.

Climate Change

Climate change has led to unpredictable rainfall patterns, reduced snowmelt, and more frequent droughts. This has significantly affected water availability in rivers, lakes, and groundwater reserves.

Groundwater Depletion

Over-extraction of groundwater for agriculture, drinking, and industrial use has led to a drastic drop in water tables across the country. Some regions are experiencing severe groundwater depletion, with wells running dry.

Pollution

Water pollution from industrial waste, agricultural runoff, and untreated sewage has made large quantities of water unfit for consumption, further reducing the available supply of clean water.
