World Water Council

India participates in the World Water Council, a global platform that addresses water-related issues. The council advocates for sustainable water policies and practices at the international level, and India contributes to shaping global water agendas.
India plays a significant and active role in the World Water Council, contributing to global dialogues on sustainable water management and sharing its unique challenges and innovations in the water sector. As one of the world's most populous countries with diverse water needs and challenges—ranging from monsoon-dependent agriculture to urban water stress—India's participation in the Council is crucial for shaping global water policies that reflect the complexities of developing nations. Indian representatives, including government officials, experts, and organizations, engage in the World Water Forum and other Council initiatives to advocate for equitable water distribution, improved water governance, and innovative solutions for water security. India's involvement also underscores its commitment to addressing issues such as climate change impacts, groundwater depletion, and water pollution, both within its borders and on a global scale. By contributing its experiences and lessons learned, India not only influences international water discourse but also benefits from global best practices and partnerships fostered by the World Water Council, helping to advance sustainable water management practices across the country.
