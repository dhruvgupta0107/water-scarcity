Desalination Technology

• Overview: Desalination involves removing salt and other impurities from seawater to make it potable. It’s particularly useful for coastal regions where freshwater resources are limited.
• Innovations: • Innovations: Recent advancements include energy-efficient desalination processes, such as reverse osmosis using graphene membranes, solar-powered desalination, and modular desalination units that are scalable and more accessible.

Fog Harvesting

• Overview: : Fog harvesting involves capturing water droplets from fog using large mesh nets. This method is particularly effective in arid and mountainous regions where fog is frequent.
• Innovations: • Innovations: R: Improved mesh materials and designs have increased the efficiency of fog collectors, making it a viable water source for communities in areas like the Andes, Africa, and parts of India

Atmospheric Water Generation (AWG)

• Overview: AWG devices extract water from humid air, condensing it into liquid water. This technology can provide drinking water in areas with high humidity but limited freshwater sources.
• Innovations: • Innovations: Solar-powered AWG systems, hybrid AWG systems that combine dehumidification with water purification, and portable AWG devices are some recent advancements in this field

Aquifer Recharge and Managed Aquifer Recharge (MAR)

• Overview: MAR involves artificially replenishing groundwater by directing surface water into aquifers through wells, infiltration basins, or recharge ponds.
• Innovations: : Techniques like using treated wastewater for aquifer recharge, constructing recharge weirs, and creating permeable pavements to enhance infiltration are being explored to sustainably manage groundwater levels. .

Precision Agriculture

• Overview: Precision agriculture uses technology to optimize water usage in farming, ensuring that crops receive the exact amount of water they need, reducing waste.
• Innovations: Smart irrigation systems using IoT sensors, drones, and satellite data to monitor soil moisture and crop health, as well as AI-driven irrigation scheduling, are revolutionizing water use in agriculture.

Decentralized Water Systems

• Overview: Decentralized water systems treat and manage water locally, reducing dependency on large-scale infrastructure and minimizing water loss during distribution.
• Innovations: Containerized treatment plants, solar-powered mini water treatment units, and neighborhood-scale water recycling systems are enabling communities to manage their water resources more independently.

Rainwater Harvesting Innovations

• Overview: Traditional rainwater harvesting involves collecting and storing rainwater for later use. Innovative approaches have enhanced its efficiency and applicability
• Innovations: Techniques like rooftop rainwater harvesting with integrated filtration, underground storage systems, and smart rainwater harvesting systems with sensors and automated controls are making this method more effective.

Artificial Glaciers and Ice Stupas

• Overview:This method involves creating artificial glaciers or ice stupas in mountainous regions to store winter water as ice, which melts gradually in the summer, providing a steady water supply.
• Innovations: : In Ladakh, India, ice stupas have been successfully used to address seasonal water shortages, with improved designs allowing for larger and longer-lasting ice structures.

Water-Saving Technologies in Industry

• Overview: Industries are major water consumers, and innovative technologies can significantly reduce their water usage.
• Innovations: Closed-loop water systems, zero-liquid discharge (ZLD) processes, water-efficient cooling technologies, and the use of treated wastewater in industrial processes are helping industries minimize their water footprint.
